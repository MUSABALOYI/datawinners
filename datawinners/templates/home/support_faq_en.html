General Information

What is DataWinners?

DataWinners is the first completely integrated, mobile data collection service. DW enables you to collect data via SMS anywhere, with any phone. And because your DataWinners' subscription includes bulk pre-paid SMS, we eliminate time-consuming negotiations with telephone carriers and SMS Gateways.

How Does it Work

When you sign up for a DataWinners' subscription, you receive: 1) access to the DataWinners website; 2) a local telephone number in your project country and; 3) a bulk SMS package.
From the DataWinners website you:

  1. Design and build data collection questionnaires (forms) for your project
  2. Create training materials that teach your staff how to format SMS responses to your questionnaires
  3. Register data senders and subjects
  4. Create automatic reminders that send SMS messages reminding staff to send in data

In the field:
Your staff collect answers to your questionnaires and SMS the answers to the local number provided by DataWinners. The service works with any phone that can send SMS

Back at DataWinners:
You have access to view, analyze and download your data in real time. You can share your data by setting account and user permissions, enabling others in your organization to have immediate access to critical project information.

What can you do with DataWinners?

Anything. Examples of some DataWinners deployments include:
Stock monitoring (medication, mosquito nets)
Monthly activity summaries (patients seen at a health center)
Client satisfaction surveys
Sales Receipt collections
Water-point monitoring

What makes DataWinners different?

DataWinners allows you to collect data in the most challenging environments where paper based systems are costly and often fail.

Unlike mobile data collection toolkits that require configuration and software expertise, you can use DataWinners with no IT experience.

DataWinners has taken on the hardest part of SMS data collection – negotiation with telecoms. Your DataWinners subscription covers the costs of SMS data collection including a local phone number and pre-paid SMS.

Is this open source? Who is the code base available to?

An international partnership of private sector companies, academic institutions and non-governmental organizations are working collaboratively to create and manage DataWinners.com as an open-source project. Human Network International (HNI) and ThoughtWorks, Inc. lead the effort to make this mobile data collection service responsive to the needs of development organizations worldwide.

DataWinners hosted service is one of the first applications built using the Mangrove data collection framework, itself an open source project that develops core tools for mobile data collection, aggregation, analysis, and visualization targeting SMS, Web, J2ME and Android via XForms. The Mangrove initiative is collaboration of ThoughtWorks, Inc, the Modi Research Lab at Columbia University and HNI.

The goal of these open-sourced projects is to create a global community, working together to make mobile data collection better. The entire code base for DataWinners and Mangrove is available for download here. We encourage partners to contribute code and ideas so DataWinners and Mangrove continue to innovate.

Data Senders

Who is a Data Sender?

A person who sends in data using SMS or Internet is a Data Sender. With DataWinners you give permission to people to send in data by first registering your Data Senders. Then DataWinners will recognize Data Senders by their telephone numbers or email addresses.

Should I Register Data Senders?

Yes. Registration is required. During registration you will be asked to include the telephone number that the Data Sender will use to send SMS. When registered Data Senders SMS in data, DataWinners will recognize the registered telephone number and accept the data. Any SMS from a non-registered number will be rejected and the Data Sender will receive an automatic error message.

Do I need to register my Data Sender's telephone number in a specific format?

Yes. When registering a Data Sender, include the country code in the telephone number and omit all spaces and special characters. For example, in Madagascar, the phone number 033 12 345 67 would be registered as: 261331234567.

Do I need to register my Data Sender's telephone number in a specific format?

Yes. When registering a Data Sender, include the country code in the telephone number and omit all spaces and special characters. For example, in Madagascar, the phone number 033 12 345 67 would be registered as: 261331234567.

Can a Data Sender self-register from the field using SMS?

No. DataWinners will not accept submissions from non-registered telephones. However, a registered Data Sender can register a new Data Sender in the system by sending in the following SMS (omit all <>):

Reg .t reporter .n .m .l .g (GPS Coordinates are optional)

Do I need to register my Data Sender for each new project that I create?

No. Once registered, a Data Sender is able to send in data for all current and future projects. However, Data Senders receive reminder messages only for projects for which they are associated. You can associate a Data Sender to a particular project by first clicking on the Data Sender link at the top of the page. Then select the data senders you want associated with your project. Then, choose the “Add to project” option from the drop down list in the “Actions” box at the top of the page. Finally choose which projects you’d like to associate your Data Senders with.

Does my 'Data Sender' need to include his or her unique ID when submitting data?

No. DataWinners will identify each Data Sender by their registered phone number.

What are success and error messages?

Each time a Data Sender submits data, DataWinners will evaluate the SMS and automatically send back a message. If the SMS contains any errors, DataWinners will send back an error message specifying the mistake made. If the SMS contains no errors, then DataWinners will send a success message back along with a summary of the submitted answers. The Data Sender can then review the summary and re-submit in case of any inadvertent errors.

How do I change the language of the success and error messages that my Data Senders receive?

Choose the Success and error message language on the Define Project step. To change the language of these messages while still in the Test State of the project, click the "Edit this Project" link from the project overview page and choose the new language. Once a project is in its Active state, the message language is fixed and cannot be changed.

What languages can my Data Senders receive their Success and Error messages in?

In the current release of DataWinners, Success and Error messages can be sent in English, French and Malagasy.

Can I customize the Success and Error messages or add a new language or dialect?

In the current release of DataWinners, Success and Error Messages are not editable or customizable. However, we plan to implement these features in upcoming releases of DataWinners.



Subjects

What is a 'Subject'?

A Subject is what your questionnaire is about; a specific person, place, thing or event on which Data Senders report.

In many cases, the Data Sender interacts with a subject, then sends in an individual report about the interaction:

A doctor examines a baby. In this case, the subject is the baby.
A supervisor measures the quality of care at a clinic. The subject is the clinic
An engineer measures the flow rate of a water pump. The subject is the water pump.
A sales person sells products to a retailer: The subject is the retailer.

What is the difference between a Summary Report and an Individual Report about a specific subject?

Data collection projects can be grouped into two broad categories: 1) Summary Reports and; 2) Individual Reports about a specific subject . With DataWinners, you can collect data for either, or both.

Summary Reports
Examples:

  • A young woman answers polling questions
    • (Example question: Do you have access to clean water?).

Individual Reports about a specific subject

With DataWinners, you can go beyond collecting summaries; You can collect individual reports about a specific subject. You can register people, places and things and give each a unique ID number that allows you to: 1) create detailed questionnaires about them (like a medical record); 2) create several different questionnaires about each subject (if your register a clinic, you can create one questionnaire to monitor stock and another to measure the quality of care at the clinic).

Examples:

A doctor examines a baby. In this case, the subject is the baby.
A supervisor measures the quality of care at a clinic. The subject is the clinic
An engineer measures the flow rate of a water pump. The subject is the water pump.

In each case, the Data Senders (in other words, people in the field) must first identify the specific “subject” (baby/clinic/water pump).

For example, the Data Sender would first answer the question: “What baby are you reporting on?”

All subsequent questions would be about the baby. (Example: “How much did the baby weigh?”)

To facilitate data collection and improve accuracy, you can register each individual Subject in DataWinners prior to collecting data about them. Each Subject will receive a unique identification number. Data Senders then use the Subject’s unique ID when they report on the results of a visit to (or from) the Subject.

Register each Subject with their geographical location and DataWinners will display each subject individually on a map.

What is the difference between a Subject Type and an Individual Subject?

A Subject Type is a general label. For example, you create the Subject Type "Clinic." You could then register the following individual Subjects:

  • North Clinic
  • South Clinic
  • East Clinic
  • West Clinic

What are the advantages to register 'Subjects' for my projects?

To collect individual reports about specific subjects, you have two choices.

  1. Each time, you can ask your Data Senders to send in background information about the subject (Example: What is the baby’s name? Where does the baby live?)
Or
  1. Register the background information about the subject first, then going forward have your Data Senders identify each subject using a unique number (example: “What is the unique identification number of the baby?” Answer: bab072)

There are three important advantages to using unique identification numbers:

  1. Improve the quality of your data. Data Senders frequently make spelling mistakes (Example: A Data Sender spells a baby’s name “Avery” during the first visit, then “Avary” during the second). When spelling mistakes happen, you have to “clean” your data by determining if “Avery” and “Avary” are two different babies, or the same baby. Mistakes happen less frequently with unique identification numbers.
  2. Make data collection easier by reducing the number of questions. It’s easier for your Data Senders to answer one question (what is the baby’s identification number?) rather than, say, four background questions (1. What is the baby’s name? 2. Where does the baby live? 3. How old is the baby? 4. What is the mother’s name?)
  3. Once subjects are registered in DataWinners, you can collect a variety of data about them. You may want to collect several different individual reports for one subject (example: a report when the baby is seen at a nutritional center and a second report when the community agent visits the baby at home). After registering the baby with DataWinners, your Data Senders can use the baby’s unique identification number for multiple questionnaires.

If you want to register your subjects with DataWinners, the first step is to identify the subject type (Example: Patient, Clinic, Water Pump).

Subjects are People (Patient, Farmer, Employee); Places (Region, District, Commune); Things (Health Center, Retail Shop, Water Pump) or Events (Training, Performance, Public Gathering)

You can choose an existing Subject Type already registered with your DataWinners account, or specify a new Subject Type.

Once you choose a subject type (example: Baby), you will see this question appear in your questionnaire builder: “What baby are you reporting on?”.

In a later step, you will:

  1. Create your subject registration questionnaire (with the background questions you want for each subject)
  2. Register a list of your subjects with DataWinners so each is assigned a unique identification number.

Can a Data Sender register a subject from the field using SMS?

Yes. Data Senders can register new Subjects in the field. Use the following format (omit all <>):

Reg .t <type of subject> .n <name of Subject> .m <mobile phone number of Subject> .l <location of Subject> .g <gps coordinates of Subject>



Data Analysis

What do the pins on the map represent?

On any map, the pins represent either the registered Data Senders or the registered Subjects. If the project type is a Summary Report, then each pin on the map corresponds to one registered Data Sender. For Individual Reports about a Specific Subject, each pin on the map corresponds to one registered Subject.

Why aren’t all of my registered Data Senders or Subjects on the map?

Only Data Senders and Subjects registered with their geographic location are displayed on the map.

If you do not see a Data Sender or Subject on the map, it is likely that an error occurred when entering its coordinates or location during registration. Please review the registered Subject/Data Sender's GPS coordinates via the All Subjects/All Data Senders lists.

How do I add or delete a record that a Data Sender has submitted?

Add a record via the "Web Submission" link from the "Data" tab for any project. To delete a data record, click on the "All Data Records" page for the project, select the data record you wish to delete by clicking on the corresponding box in the left-most column, and select "Delete" from the Actions drop down menu.

What does the 'Submission Status' column refer to on the "All Data Records" page?

The Submission Status column indicates whether an incoming SMS was error free and accepted by the system, or contained one or more errors and was rejected by the system. The term "True" indicates the SMS submission was error free and accepted by the system. The term "False" indicates the SMS submission contained errors and was rejected by the system.

The intention of displaying the "False" submissions is to serve as a diagnostic tool to help you identify what issues or problems your Data Senders are having when they send in their data.

What does the "Deleted Record" column refer to on the "All Data Records" page?

The Deleted Record column indicates whether a data record was deleted by the user. The deleted record still appears in the “All Data Records” page, but does not appear on the “Analysis” page.

The term "False" indicates that the data record has not been deleted, and is therefore included in the calculations/aggregations on the analysis page. The term "True" indicates that the record has been deleted and is therefore not included in the calculations/aggregations on the analysis page.

What is the SMS interface built around?

DataWinners uses Vumi, an open source messaging transport created by the Praekelt Foundation for core messaging services. Vumi can be interfaced with any number of SMS gateway providers and Mobile Network Operators (MNOs). In Madagascar, DataWinners integrates directly with Airtel via SMPP (as supported by Vumi). We also have integrations with gateway providers in India and Clickatell in the US.

How does DataWinners interface with local providers?

We sign agreements with mobile operators and SMS aggregators to connect DataWinners to every cell phone in the world. This means in a matter of a days, we can activate DataWinners in any country in the world.
